4 Colors

Genre: Card, Strategy, Multiplayer, Casual

Platform: Browser (HTML5)

Player Mode: SinglePlayer / Multiplayer

At first glance, it’s simple — match a color or match a number. But the real battle isn’t in the matching. It’s in the timing. Play a powerful card too early and you expose yourself. Hold it too long and you risk being forced into a defensive spiral. Every turn shifts momentum. A well-timed skip can freeze a comeback. A sudden color change can reset the board’s rhythm entirely. The tension rises when hands shrink and someone forgets to declare their final card. Victory often belongs to the player who controls the pace, not just the cards.

4 Colors revolves around hand management and turn sequencing. Players must discard cards that match either the color or the number shown in the central pile, gradually reducing their hand size.

Strategic depth emerges through special action cards that alter turn order, force draws, or change active colors. Anticipating opponents’ remaining cards becomes essential, especially during late-game pressure scenarios. The gameplay remains accessible, yet competitive layers form quickly.
